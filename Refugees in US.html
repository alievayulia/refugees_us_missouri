FLEEING HOME: REFUGEES IN THE UNITED STATES

The refugee crisis became an issue which the whole world is concerned about. Wars, conflicts, political pressure forced people from different places to flee their homes in order to save their lives. These years, resettlement of Syrian refugees created a controversy in the United States. In fact, it is not the first time when people from particular country are seeking their shelter in America.

By Iuliia Alieva | November 14, 2016

From what countries refugees arrived to US, 2002-2016

According to the data from Bureau of Population, Refugees, and Migration at the Department of State, the first 10 countries where the largest numbers of refugees in US came from are Myanmar, Iraq, Somalia, Bhutan, Cuba, Iran, Democratic Republic of Congo, Ukraine, Russia and Sudan. Syria is on the 16th position in the list with the number of 15403 refugees arrived.

What states welcomed the most refugees, 2002-2016

Missouri is among first five states, which welcomed the biggest number of refugees. The least number came in Wyoming (6), Montana (92) and Mississippi (104)
